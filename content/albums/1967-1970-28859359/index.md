Released just three years after the band’s official break-up, “The Blue Album” put The Beatles’ later-year tracks into one package (released simultaneously with “The Red Album”) featuring key songs from the group’s albums as well as stand-alone singles. With music that signified the group’s own development as well as the culture around them, this selection of material from albums such as <I>Sgt. Pepper</I> and <I>Abbey Road</I> contains some of their most radical studio experiments and most loved songs.
